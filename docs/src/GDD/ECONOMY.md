# GDD-006: The Dual-Currency Loop & Economy

## 1. Overview
The OPERATOR economy revolves around the **Mission Paradox**: the constant tension between funding immediate biological expansion (Biomass/Gel/Cash) and repairing the infrastructure for long-term survival (Scrap/Metal). 

## 2. The $100 Seed Money (The Elder's Gift)
Because of the "Cold Start" problem, the Astronaut begins with a fixed budget.
*   **Initial Bank**: `$100` (representing the Astronaut's remaining emergency credits or initial salvage).
*   **The Fail-safe (Elder's Gift)**: If the Astronaut is bankrupt and roster-wiped, the planet's immune system (The Elder) provides a 0-cost starter Slime to reboot the operation.

## 3. The Dual-Currency System
### 3.1 Biomass (Gel / $)
The immediate, liquid currency of the operation. Represents the biological energy required to interface with the local ecosystem.
*   **Usage:**
    *   **The Draft (Recruitment):** `$25` per biological entity (Slime). This cost represents the energy required to "unionize" a local biological form into the Astronaut's command structure.
    *   **The Gear Market:** `$15` per basic loadout modification (Heavy Vest, Scout Fins, Data Lens) in the Cargo Bay. Used to offset bad RNG in recruit stats or fine-tune specialists.
*   **Acquisition:** Awarded primarily through successful Mission completions in "Biomass-heavy" zones (e.g., Foraging, Trade).

### 3.2 Scrap (Metal)
The long-term infrastructure currency. Represents salvaged technological components.
*   **Usage:**
    *   **Ship Repair:** Used to restore the ship's Hull Integrity and unlock Tech Tree nodes (e.g., bringing Life Support, Nav, and Comm Arrays back online).
*   **Acquisition:** Awarded through high-risk "Dungeon" or "Excavation" missions. Scrap often requires bringing specialized engineering Slimes (Orange/Amber cultures).

## 4. The Mission Paradox (The Shepherd's Dilemma)
The core gameplay loop forces a choice with every mission completion:
1.  **Reinvest in the Organism (Spend Gel):** Buy more Slimes, expand the roster, buy Gear. This increases mission success rate but leaves the ship vulnerable as its integrity decays.
2.  **Repair the Ship (Spend Scrap):** Fix the actual win-condition parameters (Life Support, Tech Tier). This advances the overarching goal but might mean passing on crucial biological upgrades for the Slimes.
3.  **The Squeeze:** Hull integrity constantly decays. The Astronaut must execute the "Double-Tap": dispatching a team for Scrap to fix the ship, while sending a second team for Gel to replace KIA Slimes.
